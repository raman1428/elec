Basic Electronic Components & Instruments

Unit-I: Electrical Signals and Parameters

Electrical signals are used to convey information in electronic circuits. They can be in the form of voltage, current, or power.

  • Current: The flow of electric charge. Unit: Amperes (A).
  • Voltage: The potential difference that drives electric current. Unit: Volts (V).
  • Wattage: The rate at which electrical energy is transferred. Unit: Watts (W). Calculation: \(W = V \times I\).
  • AC and DC: AC periodically reverses direction, while DC flows in one direction only.

Unit-II: Serial and Parallel Connections

Components can be connected in series or parallel.

  • Series Connection: Components connected one after the other. Same current flows through all components.
  • Parallel Connection: Components connected between the same two points. Same voltage across all components.
  • Lead and Lag Connections: Used in circuits to control phase shifts.

Unit-III: Voltmeters and Ammeters

These instruments measure voltage and current.

  • Voltmeter: Measures voltage between two points. Connected in parallel.
  • Ammeter: Measures current flowing through a circuit. Connected in series.
  • Multimeter: Measures voltage, current, resistance, and more. Versatile for troubleshooting.

Unit-IV: Visualization of Electrical Signals

An oscilloscope displays electrical signals over time.

  • Introduction to Oscilloscope: Used for troubleshooting and analyzing waveforms.
  • Applications: Monitoring signal integrity, debugging circuits.
